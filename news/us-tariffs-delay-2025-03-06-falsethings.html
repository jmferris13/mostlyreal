False News from Quiz: US Delays Tariffs on Mexican and Canadian Goods

FALSE NEWS #1
"President Trump said, 'Mexico and Canada better keep kissing my you know what if they want these new tariffs to go away' during a press conference on the trade policy."

This statement is false. The article does not quote President Trump making any such crude or threatening comment about Mexico and Canada during a press conference or any other forum.

What the article does state is that "President Trump announced he would pull back some tariffs on Mexico" after Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick mentioned a possible extension of the tariff reprieve beyond automakers. There is no mention of Trump holding a press conference on this issue or making derogatory statements about Mexico and Canada.

While the article does describe tension between the U.S. and its trading partners, including Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent's negative comments about Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau, it does not attribute any vulgar ultimatums to President Trump himself.

The quote "if you want to live, treat me good" is indeed from Peter Tosh's song "Steppin' Razor," but there is no evidence in the article that Trump quoted these lyrics or made such threatening remarks to American automakers regarding the tariff delay.
